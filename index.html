Floating Energy

Role: Team leader

This project harness the energy of the sun and converts it to electrical energy. The solar panels will absorb sunlight with photovoltaic cells generating Direct Current energy. The Peltier will convert the heat energy to electrical energy. The electrical energy harvested will than be converted into usable Alternating Current energy with the help of a power inverter.

Currently we are collaborating with The ECOmpany in improving and to futher develop our prototype. We have plans to commercialize this idea, to help reduce carbon emission.

Walk Free Osteoarthritis

Role: Mechanical Lead

Osteoarthritis is a degenerative disease that most commonly occurs to old people. Osteoarthritis mostly affects the cartilage which is the hard but slippery tissue that covers the ends of the bones where they meet to form a joint. We decide to target the knee as it is an important part of the body we use and people are discouraged to walk due to the pain and are afraid to get surgery to fix it.

We designed a knee brace that assists the user in walking. Using a electromagnetic clutch, we are able to achieve to modes of usage auto and manual mode.

  • Date: March 2016 - January 2018

Solar Dehydrator

Role: Mechanical and Team Lead

For Design Thinking Project III, our team designed, built, and tested a functional, off-grid solar-powered food dehydrator. We validated its core function by achieving a 15.1% dehydration efficiency using only solar power and a fan, successfully removing 27g of water in under two hours. The project encompassed full-system engineering—from solar panel sizing and thermal analysis to prototyping with custom 3D-printed parts—resulting in a cost-optimized prototype that effectively extends food shelf life using renewable energy.

  • Date: January 2025 - May 2025
